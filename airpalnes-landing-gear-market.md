<p><p>Airplanes landing gear consists of two main types: main landing gear and nose landing gear. </p><p>Main landing gear supports the majority of the aircraft's weight and is located under the wings near the fuselage. It typically consists of two or more wheels and shock absorbers to cushion the impact of landing. </p><p>Nose landing gear is located under the front of the aircraft and supports the weight of the nose during landing and taxiing. It typically consists of a single wheel and shock absorber. Both types play crucial roles in ensuring safe landings and takeoffs for aircraft.</p></p>
