<p><p>The sequencing reagents market can be categorized into three types: Sanger sequencing, Next Generation Sequencing (NGS), and Third Generation Sequencing. Sanger sequencing is an older method that involves DNA replication and sequencing, commonly used for small-scale projects. NGS, on the other hand, uses massively parallel sequencing technology allowing faster and cheaper sequencing of large genomes. Third Generation Sequencing is the latest approach that offers even faster and high-throughput sequencing, with single molecule sequencing technologies. These advancements in sequencing technologies have revolutionized genomics research and have various applications in biomedical and clinical fields.</p></p>
