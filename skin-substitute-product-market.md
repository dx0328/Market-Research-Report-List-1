<p><p>The skin substitute product market includes products derived from animal and human sources. Animal-derived skin substitutes are made from tissues such as porcine or bovine collagen, while human-derived substitutes are typically sourced from cadavers or stem cells. Both types of substitutes are used in wound healing and skin graft procedures. Animal-derived substitutes are cost-effective and readily available, while human-derived substitutes offer more biocompatibility and potential for integration with the patient's own tissue. Both types play a crucial role in the field of regenerative medicine.</p></p>
