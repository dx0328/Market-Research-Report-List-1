<p><p>Consumer-grade 3D printers are available in two main types: FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) and SLA (Stereolithography) technology markets. FDM printers create objects by melting and depositing filament layers, allowing for versatility and cost-effectiveness, making them popular among hobbyists. On the other hand, SLA printers use UV lasers to cure resin layers, resulting in higher precision and smoother finishes, making them suitable for intricate and detailed prints. Both technologies cater to the needs of consumers interested in affordable, user-friendly 3D printing for personal use and small-scale projects.</p></p>
