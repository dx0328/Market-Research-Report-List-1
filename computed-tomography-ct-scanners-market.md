<p><p>Computed Tomography (CT) scanners are medical imaging devices used to obtain detailed cross-sectional images of the body. They are categorized into low-slice scanners (below 64 slices), medium-slice scanners (64 slices), and high-slice scanners (above 64 slices) based on the number of X-ray beam slices they can capture simultaneously. Low-slice scanners have fewer imaging capabilities and are generally more affordable. Medium-slice scanners provide better image resolution and can capture more detailed images. High-slice scanners offer the highest image resolution and are utilized in complex medical procedures necessitating highly precise imaging.</p></p>
