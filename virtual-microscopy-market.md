<p><p>Virtual microscopy is a technology that allows for the viewing and analysis of high-resolution digital images of microscope slides on a computer screen. There are two main types of virtual microscopy markets: brightfield and fluorescence. Brightfield microscopy involves illuminating a specimen with white light, producing a dark image against a light background. In contrast, fluorescence microscopy uses specific wavelengths of light to excite fluorescent molecules in the specimen, creating a highly detailed and colorful image. Both types offer unique advantages for various research and diagnostic applications.</p></p>
