<p><p>Filtering Facepiece Respirators (FFRs) are respiratory protection devices that are commonly used in areas with air pollution or airborne hazardous substances. FFRs are divided into two main market types - those without a valve and those with a valve.</p><p>FFRs without a valve provide filtration of both incoming and outgoing air, offering protection for both the wearer and the surrounding environment. These FFRs are suitable for general use and are often used in healthcare settings.</p><p>FFRs with a valve, on the other hand, have an exhalation valve that allows for effortless breathing by reducing moisture accumulation and heat build-up. These FFRs are commonly used in industrial settings where comfort and efficient breathing are crucial.</p><p>Both types of FFRs play a significant role in providing respiratory protection, but the presence or absence of a valve differentiates their functionality and areas of application.</p></p>
