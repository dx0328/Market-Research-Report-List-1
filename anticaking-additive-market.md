<p><p>Anticaking additives are commonly used in food and industrial products to prevent the formation of lumps and maintain free-flowing properties. They can be classified into three main types based on their composition: calcium compounds, such as calcium carbonate or calcium silicate, which absorb moisture and prevent clumping; silicon dioxide, which acts as a coating agent to repel moisture and prevent caking; and other additives, such as magnesium carbonate or potassium ferrocyanide, which also help to maintain product texture and flowability.</p></p>
