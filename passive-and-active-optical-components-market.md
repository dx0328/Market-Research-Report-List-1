<p><p>Passive optical components are devices that do not require external power to function and include components like splitters, couplers, filters, and wavelength division multiplexers. They play a crucial role in distributing and managing optical signals efficiently in communication networks. On the other hand, active optical components require an external power source to operate and include components like transmitters, receivers, modulators, and optical amplifiers. These components are responsible for generating, converting, and amplifying optical signals, contributing to the overall performance and functionality of optical communication systems.</p></p>
