<p><p>Single-stage tensioners are designed for simpler applications, providing a fixed tension load with a single hydraulic stage. These tensioners are typically more cost-effective and easier to use. On the other hand, multi-stage tensioners are designed for more complex applications, offering adjustable tension loads with multiple hydraulic stages. These tensioners provide greater control and precision but are usually more expensive and require more expertise to operate. Both types of tensioners play a crucial role in maintaining the integrity and performance of wind turbines.</p></p>
