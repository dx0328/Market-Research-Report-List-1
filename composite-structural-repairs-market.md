<p><p>Composite structural repairs refer to the process of fixing damaged composite materials such as carbon fiber or fiberglass used in various industries. The market for these repairs can be broken down into different types: Hand Lay-up, where layers of resin and fibers are applied manually; Vacuum Infusion, which involves vacuum pressure to infuse resin into the fibers; Autoclave, where the repaired material is placed in a special oven for heat and pressure treatment; and Others, which may include additional methods like filament winding or resin transfer molding. These methods are used to ensure strong and durable repairs to composite structures.</p></p>
