<p><p>Sterility testing is a crucial process to ensure the absence of viable microorganisms in pharmaceutical products. There are different types of sterility testing methods available in the market. Direct inoculation involves placing the product directly into a culture medium to detect any microbial growth. Membrane filtration involves passing the product through a membrane, which retains any microorganisms and allows them to grow on a culture medium. Other methods include fluid thioglycollate medium, which tests for both aerobic and anaerobic microorganisms, and the most probable number method, which determines the number of microorganisms present based on statistical calculations.</p></p>
