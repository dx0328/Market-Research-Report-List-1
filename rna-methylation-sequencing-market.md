<p><p>RNA methylation sequencing is a technique used to examine RNA modifications, specifically methylation, which plays a vital role in gene expression and cellular functions. Different types of sequencing methods are employed in this market. Radioisotope incorporation utilizes radioactive molecules to label methylated RNA for analysis. Thin-layer chromatography separates methylated RNA based on their chemical properties. Mass spectrometry identifies and quantifies methylated RNA molecules. Differential enzyme/chemical-RNA interactions detect methylation patterns using specific enzymes or chemicals. Bisulphite RNA sequencing identifies RNA methylation by treating with bisulphite to convert unmethylated cytosine. Antibody-based sequencing employs antibodies that specifically detect methylated RNA. Finally, Big Data market involves the storage, management, and analysis of the vast amount of data generated by RNA methylation sequencing experiments.</p></p>
