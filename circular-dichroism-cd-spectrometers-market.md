<p><p>Circular Dichroism (CD) Spectrometers are used to analyze the optical activity of molecules. Linearly Polarized Light Sources emit light with vibrations in a single plane, allowing analysis of anisotropy. Circularly Polarized Light Sources emit light with rotational vibrations, enabling more accurate measurement of CD. Multiple Light Sources spectrometers employ various light sources to provide a wider spectral range for analysis. These different types cater to different research needs and offer varying levels of accuracy and versatility in analyzing molecular structures and chiral characteristics.</p></p>
