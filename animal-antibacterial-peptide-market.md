<p><p>Animal antibacterial peptides are small proteins that play a significant role in the innate immune system of animals. These peptides are classified into two main types - anionic peptides and cationic peptides. Anionic peptides carry a negative charge, while cationic peptides carry a positive charge. Anionic peptides are particularly effective against Gram-positive bacteria, while cationic peptides are effective against both Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria. These peptides have gained attention in the market due to their potential as alternatives to conventional antibiotics in combating bacterial infections in animals.</p></p>
