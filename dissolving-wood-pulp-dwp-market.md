<p><p>Dissolving Wood Pulp (DWP) is a type of wood pulp specifically produced for use in the production of viscose and other cellulose-based products. Two main types of DWP are Alkali Wood Pulp (also known as Sulfate Wood Pulp) and Wood Pulp of Sulfite. Alkali Wood Pulp is produced using the sulfate process, which involves treating wood chips with a mixture of chemicals. Wood Pulp of Sulfite, on the other hand, is produced using the sulfite process, which involves using sulfurous acid to break down wood fibers. Both types of DWP have their own unique characteristics and uses in various industries.</p></p>
