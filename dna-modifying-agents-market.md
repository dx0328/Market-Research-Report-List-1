<p><p>DNA modifying agents are classified into different types based on their mechanisms of action. Cross-linkers are molecules that create covalent bonds between DNA strands, leading to the formation of cross-links. Enzymes, on the other hand, are proteins that catalyze specific reactions on DNA molecules, such as cutting or ligating DNA strands. Both cross-linkers and enzymes play crucial roles in genetic engineering, research, and various biotechnological applications by modifying DNA sequences for specific purposes. These agents are essential tools in molecular biology and biotechnology research.</p></p>
