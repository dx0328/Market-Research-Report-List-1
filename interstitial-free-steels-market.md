<p><p>Interstitial free steels are classified based on their production process as hot rolled and cold rolled. Hot rolled interstitial free steels are produced at high temperatures, making them easier to form and shape. These steels are commonly used in applications that require a higher level of ductility. On the other hand, cold rolled interstitial free steels are processed at lower temperatures, resulting in increased strength and surface finish. They are preferred for applications where a smoother surface and tighter tolerances are required.</p></p>
