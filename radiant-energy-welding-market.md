<p><p>Radiant energy welding refers to the process of using laser or electron beams to join materials together. Laser beam welding involves focusing a high-energy laser beam onto the materials, causing them to melt and fuse together. This method is widely used in various industries, including automotive and aerospace. On the other hand, electron beam welding employs a high-velocity electron beam that generates heat upon striking the materials, resulting in their fusion. The electron beam welding market offers highly efficient and precise joining solutions, particularly for applications involving deep weld penetrations.</p></p>
